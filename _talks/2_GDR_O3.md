Invited to deliver the closing workshop of the GDR O3 Annual Meeting, I introduced researchers to the principles of coffee sensory science through an interactive tasting session. Participants explored how genetics, terroir, processing, roasting, and brewing shape flavour, while learning to apply the Specialty Coffee Association's Coffee Value Assessment (CVA) framework to evaluate and communicate sensory experiences. The workshop highlighted coffee as a model system for studying olfaction, multisensory perception, and flavour cognition, bridging contemporary sensory neuroscience with professional sensory practice.
