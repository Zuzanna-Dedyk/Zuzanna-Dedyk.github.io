# AR Napping Table
### From Static Sensory Maps to Dynamic Perceptual Behaviour

Sensory science has traditionally focused on the final outcomes of perception: which products are considered similar, which attributes define a category, and how individuals organise sensory experiences. However, these final representations provide only a snapshot of the perceptual process. The exploration, uncertainty, revisions, and decision dynamics that shape these representations remain largely invisible.

This project develops and validates a novel Augmented Reality (AR) Napping platform to transform projective mapping from a static measurement into a dynamic behavioural assessment. By integrating sensory evaluation with computer vision and real-time spatial tracking, the platform captures not only where participants place products, but also how they construct their perceptual maps.

Building on the established Napping® methodology, participants organise specialty cold brew coffee samples in a two-dimensional sensory space according to perceived similarity. The AR system preserves the ecological validity and intuitive interaction of traditional Napping while continuously recording the temporal and spatial dynamics of the sorting process, including exploration trajectories, product relocations, hesitation patterns, and stabilisation strategies.

This project validates whether AR-based Napping produces perceptual representations equivalent to conventional paper-based methods while providing access to previously inaccessible behavioural markers of sensory decision-making. Through the combination of augmented reality, computer vision, and computational analysis, it introduces a new framework for process-sensitive sensory evaluation — shifting the field from analysing only the final perceptual map towards understanding how it is constructed.

Beyond sensory science, this approach provides a methodological bridge between cognitive neuroscience and naturalistic human behaviour, offering new opportunities to investigate the mechanisms underlying categorisation, expertise, and perceptual organisation.

**Methods:** Augmented Reality, projective mapping (Napping®), computer vision, behavioural tracking, sensory evaluation, trajectory analysis, computational modelling.
