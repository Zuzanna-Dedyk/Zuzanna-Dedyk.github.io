# The Art and Analytics of Wine Tasting

### How does expertise transform sensory experience into structured knowledge?

Flavour perception is not a simple readout of sensory input. It emerges from the interaction between taste, smell, memory, language, cultural learning, and prior experience. This project investigates how the human brain organises complex sensory information into meaningful cognitive spaces, using wine tasting as a naturalistic model of expertise.

The central question is: how do experts and novices construct different internal maps of flavour? While experienced tasters often show remarkable agreement in describing and evaluating wines, beginners tend to rely on fragmented perceptual impressions. The underlying structure of these differences — the geometry of the cognitive spaces that guide perception and decision-making — remains largely unknown.

Combining sensory science, cognitive neuroscience, and computational modelling, this doctoral project maps how wine knowledge is represented and reorganised through expertise. Participants construct perceptual and semantic maps of wines using projective mapping (Napping) techniques, generating individual representations of flavour similarity. These spatial arrangements are then analysed using representational geometry and graph-based approaches to quantify the structure, organisation, and complexity of flavour spaces.

By comparing experts and novices, this research aims to reveal how learning reshapes perceptual representations: whether expertise emerges through the refinement, compression, and reorganisation of sensory dimensions into more efficient cognitive maps.

Ultimately, this work seeks to develop a mechanistic understanding of how the brain transforms complex sensory experiences into structured knowledge — providing a framework for studying expertise across domains such as wine, coffee, and other multisensory experiences.

**Methods:** Projective Mapping (Napping), sensory evaluation, representational similarity analysis, graph theory, computational modelling, expertise research.
